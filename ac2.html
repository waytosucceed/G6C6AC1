Overview of Grammar topics:

Adjectives are words that describe the qualities or states of being of nouns.

Six types of adjectives:

  • Qualitative Adjectives

  • Quantitative Adjectives

  • Demonstrative Adjectives

  • Interrogative Adjectives

  • Possessive Adjectives

  • Adjectives of numbers


Let us take a look at the table to have a quick revision of it.


Type of Adjective Usage Examples
Qualitative Adjectives It describes the qualities or characteristics of a noun.They answer questions like "What kind?" or "How is it?" beautiful, delicious, intelligent, old, small,bright
Quantitative Adjectives It indicates the quantity or amount of something. They answer questions like "how much?" or "how many?" many, few, several,ample, abundant,numerous
Demonstrative Adjectives It points out or identifies specific nouns. They answer the question "Which one?". this, that, these,those
Interrogative Adjectives They are used to ask questions. They introduce a question about a noun. which, what, whose
Possessive Adjectives It indicates ownership or possession of something. Theyanswer the question "Whose?". my, your, his, her, its,our, their
Adjectives of numbers Adjectives of numbers are used to express a specific number or an inexact quantity.
a) Cardinal Adjectives indicate a precise or exact number.
b) Ordinal Adjectives indicate the position or order in a sequence.		 a) Cardinal Adjectives:: one, two, five,ten, hundred.
b) Ordinal Adjectives: first, second, third, fourth,tenth.
c) Indefinite Adjectives: Several books, Many people, Few opportunities.
